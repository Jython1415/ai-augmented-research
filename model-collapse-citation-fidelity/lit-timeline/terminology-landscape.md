# Terminology Landscape: "Model Collapse" and Related Concepts

**Purpose**: Document the full landscape of concepts that overlap with, are confused with, or are adjacent to "model collapse" as studied by Shumailov et al. (2024). This informs our classification pipeline — classifiers need to know what this study is and is NOT about.

**Relationship to timeline.md**: The lit timeline tracks papers in the Shumailov et al. lineage (directly responding to or building on the Nature paper). This document covers the broader terminology space — related but distinct concepts, competing definitions, and metaphorical terms used in public discourse.

---

## 1. Target Concept: Model Collapse (Shumailov et al.)

**Definition**: Progressive degradation of generative models when trained on synthetic data generated by predecessor models. Distribution tails disappear (early collapse), then output converges to a narrow, near-useless distribution (late collapse).

**Key papers**: Shumailov et al. (arXiv:2305.17493, May 2023; Nature 631:755-759, Jul 2024)

**Original title**: "The Curse of Recursion: Training on Generated Data Makes Models Forget"

**Critical conditions**: Demonstrated under data *replacement* (old data discarded each generation). Data *accumulation* prevents collapse (Gerstgrasser et al., 2024).

**PDF**: `papers/shumailov-2023-curse-of-recursion.pdf`

## 2. Easily Confused Concepts

### 2a. Mode Collapse (GANs)

**Definition**: A failure mode in Generative Adversarial Networks where the generator produces only a narrow subset of the true data distribution, reducing output diversity.

**Mechanism**: Generator-discriminator arms race dynamics; generator exploits discriminator weaknesses by producing only modes that reliably fool it.

**How it differs from model collapse**:
- Mode collapse is a *within-generation* training dynamics problem
- Model collapse is an *across-generation* degradation from recursive synthetic data training
- Mode collapse solutions (Wasserstein GAN, mini-batch discrimination) don't address model collapse
- Shumailov et al. explicitly acknowledged borrowing "model collapse" nomenclature from GAN "mode collapse" literature, creating terminological overlap

**Confusion risk**: VERY HIGH — similar names, both reduce diversity, both involve generative models

**Key reference**: [Mode collapse - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mode_collapse)

### 2b. Neural Collapse

**Definition**: A geometric structure emerging during the terminal phase of deep learning training where within-class activation variability collapses to zero and class means converge to a simplex equiangular tight frame.

**How it differs**: Neural collapse is BENEFICIAL — associated with better generalization. It's the opposite of degradation.

**Confusion risk**: MODERATE — same word "collapse" but opposite implications

**Key paper**: Papyan et al. "Prevalence of neural collapse during the terminal phase of deep learning training." PNAS (2020).

### 2c. Catastrophic Forgetting / Catastrophic Interference

**Definition**: Neural networks trained sequentially on new tasks rapidly forget previously learned information because parameter updates for new tasks push weights away from optima for old tasks.

**How it differs from model collapse**:
- Catastrophic forgetting is a *sequential task learning* problem (within a single model)
- Model collapse is a *recursive generation* problem (across model generations)
- Different root causes: parameter interference vs. distribution tail loss
- Different mitigations: EWC, replay buffers vs. data accumulation, verification

**Confusion risk**: MODERATE-HIGH — both involve "forgetting" and occur with new training data

**Key paper**: Kirkpatrick et al. "Overcoming catastrophic forgetting in neural networks." PNAS (2017).

### 2d. Data Contamination / Data Poisoning

**Definition**:
- Data poisoning: Deliberate injection of malicious/biased samples to manipulate model behavior (adversarial attack)
- Data contamination: Unintentional inclusion of synthetic/biased data causing performance degradation

**How it differs**: Different root cause and intentionality. Model collapse is unintentional degradation from a feedback loop; data poisoning is deliberate attack.

**Confusion risk**: HIGH — both degrade models through training data quality issues

### 2e. Model Autophagy Disorder (MAD)

**Definition**: Degradation phenomenon where generative models trained on synthetic data without sufficient fresh real data progressively lose quality or diversity across generations.

**How it differs**: It doesn't, substantially — this is essentially the same phenomenon as model collapse, discovered independently with different framing.

**Key paper**: Alemohammad et al. "Self-Consuming Generative Models Go MAD." arXiv:2307.01850 (Jul 2023); ICLR 2024.

**Important**: This is a concurrent, independent discovery — NOT a follow-up to Shumailov. Shumailov's preprint appeared May 2023; Alemohammad's appeared July 2023. "Model collapse" became the dominant term after Shumailov's Nature publication.

**PDF**: `papers/alemohammad-2023-self-consuming-MAD.pdf`

### 2f. Knowledge Collapse

**Definition**: LLMs generating output toward the distribution center, neglecting long-tail knowledge. Conceptually related to model collapse's tail loss but framed as an information access problem rather than a training feedback loop.

**Key paper**: Peterson "AI and the Problem of Knowledge Collapse." arXiv:2404.03502 (Apr 2024); AI & Society (Springer).

**PDF**: `papers/peterson-2024-knowledge-collapse.pdf`

## 3. Broader Category: Synthetic Data Feedback Loops

Model collapse is one specific consequence within the broader category of synthetic data feedback loops. Other consequences include:

- **Fairness feedback loops**: Biased models generate biased synthetic data, amplifying discrimination
- **Model-induced distribution shift (MIDS)**: Successive model outputs shift the training distribution
- **Scaling law changes**: Synthetic data alters the relationship between dataset size and model performance

These are generally NOT confused with model collapse in casual discourse because they use different terminology.

## 4. Metaphorical Terms in Public Discourse

These terms appear frequently in social media discussion of AI training on AI data. Understanding their provenance is critical for classification.

### 4a. "Habsburg AI"

**Origin**: Coined by Jathan Sadowski on the Machine Kills podcast, February 13, 2023
**Provenance**: PREDATES Shumailov's preprint by 3 months. Coined independently.
**Meaning**: Metaphor comparing AI training on AI data to royal inbreeding — genetic (informational) degradation from a closed loop
**Tied to Shumailov?**: NO — coined before the paper existed. Used broadly for recursive AI training concerns.
**Source**: [X post by Sadowski](https://x.com/jathansadowski/status/1625245803211272194)

### 4b. "AI ouroboros"

**Origin**: February 2023, Multiple sources (Shafiqah Othman on Medium, others)
**Provenance**: PREDATES Shumailov's preprint. General mythological metaphor applied to AI.
**Meaning**: Self-consuming serpent metaphor — AI consuming its own output in a loop
**Tied to Shumailov?**: NO — emerged independently. Used for multiple AI concerns (self-modification, truth degradation, training loops).

### 4c. "Digital inbreeding"

**Origin**: 2023-2024 press coverage
**Provenance**: Emerged alongside model collapse discourse, more closely tied to the arXiv preprint than the Nature paper
**Meaning**: Same as Habsburg AI but without the historical reference
**Tied to Shumailov?**: LOOSELY — appears in press coverage of the paper but not coined by or for it

### 4d. "AI eating itself" / "AI feeding on itself"

**Origin**: General metaphor, pre-2024
**Provenance**: Not tied to any specific paper. Applied to model collapse, misinformation loops, ad targeting, information integrity.
**Tied to Shumailov?**: NO — general metaphor used across many AI concerns
**Note**: TechCrunch headline "Model collapse: scientists warn against letting AI eat its own tail" (Jul 2024) fused this metaphor with the Nature publication

### 4e. "Self-consuming generative models"

**Origin**: Alemohammad et al. paper title, July 2023
**Provenance**: Technical term from a DIFFERENT paper (MAD paper, not Shumailov)
**Tied to Shumailov?**: NO — from Alemohammad et al.'s concurrent, independent work

## 5. The Definitional Crisis (Mar 2025)

Schaeffer et al. "Position: Model Collapse Does Not Mean What You Think" (arXiv:2503.03150, Mar 2025) identified **eight distinct definitions** of "model collapse" in the literature, falling into three families:

1. **Test loss behavior** on real data over iterations
2. **Deformation of real data distribution** over iterations (Shumailov's primary framing)
3. **Scaling behavior** of test loss with data amount

**Implication for our study**: The same experimental setup can show "collapse" or "no collapse" depending on which definition is used. This means:
- Casual discourse conflating these definitions is expected and explainable
- Our classification must be aware that "model collapse" in a post could refer to any of 8 things
- Only posts with traceable citation chains to Shumailov et al. can be confidently attributed

**PDF**: `papers/schaeffer-2025-does-not-mean-what-you-think.pdf`

## 6. Implications for Classification

### What this study IS about
Posts that demonstrably cite, reference, or engage with Shumailov et al. (2024) specifically — via:
- Direct links to the Nature article or arXiv preprint
- Naming the authors (Shumailov, Shumaylov)
- Quoting the paper title
- Replying to or quoting a post that does any of the above
- Sharing identifiable news coverage of the paper

### What this study is NOT about
- General "model collapse" discourse without traceable citation
- Habsburg AI / AI ouroboros / other metaphors (unless in a citation chain)
- Mode collapse in GANs
- Catastrophic forgetting
- Data poisoning
- MAD / self-consuming models (unless explicitly citing Shumailov)
- Neural collapse
- General AI pessimism that happens to use collapse language
